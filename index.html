Stationery & Uniforms

New term, new uniform

Each January and September, Msingi fits children heading back to school with a full uniform, shoes, and a term's worth of exercise books — often the difference between attending and staying home.

"My daughter stopped hiding her old shoes under the desk." — Parent, Ongata Rongai
Widows' Nutrition

A monthly basket, not a one-off

540 widows across Kajiado receive a monthly food basket of maize, beans, cooking oil, and vegetables, distributed through a rotating network of community elders who know each household by name.

"I no longer choose between medicine and dinner." — Programme recipient, age 68
Boychild Recovery

Counselling, then a trade

The Boychild programme pairs young men recovering from drug dependency with a counsellor and, once stabilised, a mentor in a trade — welding, boda repair, or carpentry — so recovery leads somewhere.

"They didn't just get me clean, they got me a chair at the workshop." — Programme graduate, 22
