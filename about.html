About

Why Msingi exists.

Msingi started in 2021 when three teachers in Kajiado County pooled their own salaries to buy uniforms for children being sent home for lacking them. What began as an informal fund is now a registered trust — still run by people who grew up in the communities it serves.

What we believe

Three commitments that shape every program decision.

01

Transparency by default

Every gift is logged the day it lands. Our public ledger and annual audits exist so no one has to take our word for it.

02

Dignity over spectacle

We don't photograph people at their lowest moment for a campaign. Stories are shared with consent, and often in the recipient's own words.

03

Local-first delivery

Field officers, elders, and mentors are drawn from the communities we work in — they're neighbours first, staff second.

How we got here

Five years, three programs.

2021

An informal fund among teachers

Three primary school teachers in Kajiado begin quietly covering uniform costs for pupils being turned away.

2022

Registered as a trust; Widows' Nutrition launches

Msingi Community Trust is formally registered. The first monthly food baskets reach 60 widows in Kajiado Central.

2023

School Essentials Fund formalised

The uniform fund becomes a standing program with its own budget line, reaching over 1,200 children in its first year.

2024

Boychild Recovery & Mentorship begins

In response to requests from mothers in the Widows' Nutrition program, Msingi launches counselling and mentorship support for young men.

2026

$4M disbursed, three programs strong

Msingi now supports 3,180 children, 540 widows, and 30 young men in recovery — with M-Pesa and PayPal giving open to anyone, anywhere.
